Website Performance Checks

Performance checks show how quickly a page loads and becomes usable. They help explain whether a website feels fast enough for customers.

Business ImpactFaster pages help more visitors reach the information they came for.

What is it?

Website performance includes page speed, image size, script weight, server response, and how quickly visitors can see and use the page. A performance score is a summary, not the whole story.

Beacon looks at the practical issues behind the score.

Why business owners care

Slow pages can make customers leave before they read your offer or contact you. Speed also affects search experience and mobile usability.

Performance work is often more cost-effective than a full redesign. The key is knowing what is actually slowing the page down.

What Beacon reviews

Beacon reviews public page performance using tools such as Google Lighthouse. It looks at page weight, images, caching, layout movement, and loading priorities.

Beacon does not guess from a score alone. It explains the likely causes behind the result.

Where the information comes from

The information comes from public page tests and browser-visible page behavior. Lighthouse is one common source.

Results can vary by network, device, and test timing, so Beacon treats performance as a guide for improvement rather than a single permanent grade.

How to read your report

A performance recommendation should show what is likely slowing the site and how much it may matter. Large images, unused scripts, and poor caching are common examples.

Focus on the changes that affect customers first, especially mobile visitors.

Common mistakes

Common misunderstandingDo not treat one warning as the whole story. Read it with the rest of the report, then confirm the recommended change with the person who manages the website.

Recommended next step

Start with image optimization, caching, and large scripts before considering a redesign. These changes often improve speed without changing the whole site.

Ask your web provider to address the highest-impact items first and retest after changes.
