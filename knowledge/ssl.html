SSL and HTTPS Checks

SSL is the older common name people use for website certificates. HTTPS is the secure browser connection that uses those certificates.

Business ImpactBrowser warnings can make visitors hesitate before they contact or buy from you.

What is it?

An SSL certificate helps prove that visitors are connecting to the website they intended to visit. It also helps protect information as it moves between the browser and the website.

Most modern websites should load over HTTPS. Browsers now expect it.

Why business owners care

Certificate problems can cause browser warnings that make customers hesitate. HTTPS also supports search visibility, payment flows, contact forms, and general trust.

A working certificate does not mean the whole website is perfect. It means one important foundation is in place.

What Beacon reviews

Beacon checks whether the website loads over HTTPS and whether the public certificate appears valid. Beacon also reviews common HTTPS settings shown by public tools.

Beacon does not attempt to break into the site. It reads the same certificate and browser connection information that public tools can see.

Where the information comes from

The information comes from browser checks and public certificate testing services such as SSL Labs. These tools inspect the public HTTPS setup.

The report reflects the setup visible at the time of the review.

How to read your report

An SSL or HTTPS note may point to an expired certificate, missing redirect, outdated setting, or a configuration that deserves review. The report should explain the likely customer impact.

Focus first on issues that create browser warnings or prevent pages from loading securely.

Common mistakes

Common misunderstandingDo not treat one warning as the whole story. Read it with the rest of the report, then confirm the recommended change with the person who manages the website.

Recommended next step

Ask your hosting provider or web developer to confirm the certificate and HTTPS redirect settings. Renew expired certificates immediately.

For lower-priority configuration warnings, schedule improvements during normal website maintenance.
