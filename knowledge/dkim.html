DKIM Records Explained

DKIM helps mail providers verify that an email was signed by a service approved for your domain. It is another trust check for business email.

Quick FactDKIM is a signing check managed by your email provider and published in DNS.

What is it?

DKIM stands for DomainKeys Identified Mail. It uses a public DNS record and a private signing key held by your email provider.

You do not need to read the key yourself. The important question is whether the public record for your sending service appears to be present.

Why business owners care

DKIM helps protect the reputation of your business email. It gives mail providers another way to confirm that a message came through an approved service.

If DKIM is not set up for a sender you use, some email may be treated with more suspicion. It is especially important for newsletters, invoices, and customer communication.

What Beacon reviews

Beacon looks for public DKIM selector records when they can be identified. A selector is the label an email provider uses to publish its DKIM record.

Beacon does not log into your email provider. It only checks records that are visible publicly.

Where the information comes from

The information comes from public DNS lookups for known or supplied DKIM selectors. Different providers use different selector names.

If a selector is unknown, Beacon may recommend confirming DKIM directly with your email provider.

How to read your report

A DKIM note may mean Beacon found a record, could not find a likely record, or needs provider confirmation. It should not be treated as proof that email is failing.

Read DKIM together with SPF and DMARC. The three checks work together to describe email trust.

Common mistakes

Common misunderstandingDo not treat one warning as the whole story. Read it with the rest of the report, then confirm the recommended change with the person who manages the website.

Recommended next step

Confirm which services send email for your domain. Then check each provider's DKIM setup instructions.

If Beacon lists a missing or uncertain DKIM record, ask your email provider or IT contact to verify the correct selector and DNS value.
