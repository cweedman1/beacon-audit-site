Cloudflare Website Checks

Cloudflare is a service many businesses use for DNS, speed, HTTPS, and website protection features. Some settings are public, while others require account access.

Beacon Looks ForPublic clues that Cloudflare affects DNS, HTTPS, caching or page delivery.

What is it?

Cloudflare can sit between visitors and your website host. It may manage DNS records, cache files, route traffic, and apply browser-facing settings.

Not every website uses Cloudflare. When it does, it becomes an important part of the website setup.

Why business owners care

Cloudflare settings can affect whether the website loads, how fast it feels, and how HTTPS behaves. They can also affect what happens during a hosting change or redesign.

If nobody knows who manages the Cloudflare account, simple changes can become difficult.

What Beacon reviews

Beacon reviews public clues that suggest Cloudflare is in use, including DNS behavior, proxy indicators, HTTPS behavior, caching hints, and public response headers.

Beacon does not log into Cloudflare or review private account settings unless a separate authorized process is arranged.

Where the information comes from

The information comes from public DNS lookups, browser inspection, response headers, and page behavior. These are visible without account access.

If Beacon cannot confirm a setting publicly, the report should recommend owner confirmation rather than guessing.

How to read your report

A Cloudflare note may mean Beacon saw signs of Cloudflare or saw a setting that should be confirmed. It does not mean the account is mismanaged.

Read the recommendation as a prompt to confirm ownership, caching behavior, DNS routing, or HTTPS settings.

Common mistakes

Common misunderstandingDo not treat one warning as the whole story. Read it with the rest of the report, then confirm the recommended change with the person who manages the website.

Recommended next step

Confirm who owns the Cloudflare account and who can make changes. Then document which records and settings are important for the website and email.

Before a redesign or host move, review Cloudflare with the vendor doing the work so important settings are not lost.
