SPF Records Explained

SPF helps mail providers understand which services are allowed to send email for your domain. It is one part of making business email easier to trust.

Beacon Looks ForWhether your public email sender list matches the services your business actually uses.

What is it?

SPF stands for Sender Policy Framework. It is a DNS record that lists the mail services approved to send email for your domain.

For example, your SPF record may include Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, your email marketing service, or another sender you use.

Why business owners care

If SPF is missing or outdated, legitimate email can be harder for mail providers to trust. If it is too broad, it can make email settings harder to understand and maintain.

SPF does not solve every email problem by itself. It works best alongside DKIM and DMARC.

What Beacon reviews

Beacon checks whether an SPF record exists and whether it appears to include the services a business likely uses. Beacon also looks for common setup issues, such as multiple SPF records.

Beacon does not send test emails from your domain. It reviews public DNS information only.

Where the information comes from

The SPF information comes from the public DNS TXT record for your domain. This is visible to mail providers and public lookup tools.

The report records the value Beacon saw during the review. Your email or DNS provider should confirm any change before it is made.

How to read your report

If Beacon flags SPF, the report will explain whether the record appears missing, duplicated, too broad, or in need of review. A note does not mean your email is broken.

It means the setup deserves a closer look before email deliverability or trust becomes harder to manage.

Common mistakes

Common misunderstandingDo not treat one warning as the whole story. Read it with the rest of the report, then confirm the recommended change with the person who manages the website.

Recommended next step

Confirm every service that sends email for your domain. Then remove senders you no longer use and keep the record aligned with your current email tools.

Review SPF together with DKIM and DMARC so the email setup is understandable as a whole.
