Continuity

Digital Continuity Checks

Digital continuity is the ability to understand, maintain, and improve your website over time. It is about avoiding confusion when vendors, tools, or business needs change.

Quick FactWebsite ownership details are easier to document before something breaks.

What is it?

Digital continuity looks at whether the website setup is understandable and maintainable. This includes domains, hosting, analytics, important third-party tools, and basic documentation.

It is less about a single score and more about whether the business can keep control of its website.

Why business owners care

Many website problems become expensive because nobody knows who owns an account, where a record lives, or why a tool was added. Clear ownership reduces that risk.

Continuity also helps when changing vendors or planning a redesign. You can make better decisions when the current setup is understandable.

What Beacon reviews

Beacon reviews public clues about website structure, domain setup, third-party services, old records, and maintainability concerns. It may also identify questions the business should answer internally.

Beacon does not access private accounts. It helps organize what can be seen publicly and what should be confirmed by the owner.

Where the information comes from

The information comes from public website pages, DNS records, browser inspection, and visible third-party scripts or services. Beacon also notes when a detail cannot be confirmed publicly.

This keeps the review honest. If something needs owner confirmation, the report should say so.

How to read your report

A continuity recommendation may ask you to document an account, confirm a provider, remove an old service, or clarify who manages a setting. These are housekeeping items, not scare items.

The value is in making future website work easier and less dependent on memory.

Common mistakes

Common misunderstandingDo not treat one warning as the whole story. Read it with the rest of the report, then confirm the recommended change with the person who manages the website.

Recommended next step

Create a simple website ownership list with the domain registrar, host, email provider, analytics tools, and key vendors. Keep it somewhere the business can access.

Then review old services and records before approving redesigns or major platform changes.
